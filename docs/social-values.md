# Social Values

*How L·A·B approaches the questions of human life that every community must answer.*

---

## Children

Children are not the future of L·A·B — they are its present.

They participate in community life from the beginning: in meditation, in forgiveness,
in caring for the environment, in acts of kindness. They are not trained for the religion;
they grow within it.

Children are expected to learn, not conform. The goal is to give them values they can
carry into a world we cannot predict — curiosity, empathy, responsibility, and an honest
relationship with the natural world.

The community commits to providing a **safe, nurturing, and inclusive environment**
in which children develop their own identities within the context of shared values.

---

## Elders

Older people are among the most valued members of the L·A·B community.

They bring experience, wisdom, and continuity — a living connection between the past
and the present. They serve as **role models**, mentors, and guardians of tradition.
They participate fully in community life: in ritual, in governance, in storytelling.

The community commits to honoring and caring for its elders, and to creating spaces
where their voices are sought and heard.

---

## Gender

L·A·B affirms the inherent worth and dignity of all individuals, regardless of gender
identity or expression.

- Gender is understood as complex, multifaceted, and personal.
- All individuals have the freedom to express their gender in the way that is true to them.
- Gender stereotypes and discrimination are actively challenged.
- All members have the right to use the pronouns and language that feel most affirming.
- Equality of access — to healthcare, education, employment, housing — is upheld unconditionally.

---

## Marriage

Marriage in L·A·B is a freely chosen commitment between individuals who love and respect each other.

It is understood as a living relationship — one that requires active work, forgiveness,
and ongoing renewal. The community supports couples in building relationships grounded
in the core values: honesty, kindness, responsibility, and compassion.

There is no prescribed form. What matters is the commitment, not the ceremony.

---

## Sexuality

L·A·B recognizes sexuality as a natural and healthy dimension of human life.

Sexual relationships should be:

- Based on **mutual respect, care, and consent**
- Entered into with a **responsible and conscious** mindset
- **Safe** and free from coercion, exploitation, or harm

All individuals have the right to make informed choices about their own sexual activity.
The community provides education, open conversation, and a non-judgmental environment
in which sexuality can be discussed honestly.

Sexual behavior that is exploitative, coercive, or harmful is not tolerated.

---

## Substance Use

L·A·B acknowledges that substances — including drugs and alcohol — exist in the world
and in the lives of its members. The community does not pretend otherwise.

The community's position:

- **Excessive, harmful, or irresponsible** use is incompatible with the values of L·A·B.
- **Moderation and mindfulness** are encouraged.
- Substance use is treated as a **health matter**, not a moral failing.
- Members struggling with substance use are offered **support, education, and compassion** —
  not shame or exclusion.

---

## Justice & Conflict

### Restorative, Not Punitive

L·A·B does not organize itself around punishment.

When harm occurs within the community, the response is **restorative**:
understanding the root cause, hearing all parties, repairing what was broken.
The goal is healing — of the harmed, of the community, and where possible, of the person who caused harm.

### Serious Harm

Where a member continues to act in ways that endanger the community, measures may be taken
to protect others. These measures are:

- Used only as a **last resort**
- Applied with **compassion and transparency**
- Guided at every step by the community's shared values

L·A·B does not support banishment or permanent exclusion as a first response.
Reconciliation is always the preferred path.

### Murder

Taking a life contradicts every principle on which L·A·B is built.

A member who commits murder is expected to face legal consequences under the laws of their society.
Within the community, the path forward — however long and difficult — involves:
acknowledging the harm, taking full responsibility, and demonstrating genuine commitment to repair.
