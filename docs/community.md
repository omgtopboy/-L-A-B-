# Community

*The communal place, how we govern ourselves, and how we sustain the work.*

---

## The Communal Place

The physical heart of every L·A·B community is its gathering space —
a building that embodies the values of the religion in its very construction.

### Setting & Construction

The communal place is nestled in a natural setting, surrounded by trees and living vegetation.
It is built from locally sourced materials — wood, stone — chosen for their connection
to the land and their low environmental footprint.

### Design Principles

| Principle | Application |
|---|---|
| **Environmental sustainability** | Passive solar design, renewable energy, composting, recycling |
| **Accessibility** | Wheelchair accessible; welcoming to all physical abilities and backgrounds |
| **Community first** | Seating arranged in circles; no stage, no hierarchy of space |
| **Flexibility** | Space reconfigures for different events, seasons, and needs |
| **Nature integration** | Gardens, green spaces, natural light throughout |
| **Local materials** | Locally sourced, recycled, or sustainably produced wherever possible |
| **Safety** | Designed with the wellbeing of all members as the primary concern |

### Infrastructure

- **Green roof** — insulation, biodiversity, connection to the sky
- **Solar panels** — reduced energy dependence; a visible commitment to sustainability
- **Water conservation systems** — rainwater collection, low-flow fixtures
- **Communal kitchen and dining area** — for weekly meals and daily gathering
- **Quiet spaces** — for meditation, reflection, and solitude
- **Community art** — walls featuring work made by members; the space as a living gallery

## Governance

L·A·B communities are governed by the members who comprise them.
There is no central authority, no permanent hierarchy, no external institution.

### Principles

**1. Democratic and participatory decision-making**
Every member has a voice. Decisions that affect the community are made with the community.
Processes are designed to ensure all voices — not only the loudest — are heard.

**2. Transparency and accountability**
Leaders report to the community, not the other way around.
Decision-making is open; reasoning is shared; outcomes are reviewed together.

**3. Inclusivity and equity**
The governance process itself must model the values it protects.
Diversity of background, experience, and perspective is actively sought, not tolerated.

**4. Environmental sustainability**
Governance decisions include environmental impact as a standing consideration —
not an afterthought.

**5. Service and altruism**
Leadership in L·A·B is a form of service. Those who lead do so for the community's benefit,
not their own advancement.

## Finance

L·A·B has no mandatory tithes, no membership fees, no financial requirements of any kind.

Financial support for the community comes entirely from **voluntary contributions**
by members who share its values and wish to sustain its work.

Funds raised are used for:
- Maintenance and sustainability of communal spaces
- Environmental care and conservation projects
- Education and community programming
- Support for members in need

Financial decisions are made transparently, in accordance with the governance principles above.

## Community Representatives

Members of the community may take on representative roles — not as titles of authority,
but as callings toward a particular kind of service.

| Role | Focus |
|---|---|
| Harmony Seeker | Conflict resolution and community cohesion |
| Virtue Spreader | Embodying and sharing the core values |
| Truth Bearer | Honesty, transparency, and accountability |
| Mercy Maker | Compassion and care for those who are struggling |
| Justice Bringer | Equity, challenge of injustice, advocacy |
| Love Ambassador | Active outreach and community-building |
| Compassionate Guide | Mentorship and spiritual support |
| Gratitude Guardian | Cultivating appreciation and positive awareness |
| Responsibility Teacher | Accountability practices and education |
| Forgiveness Advocate | Facilitating reconciliation and healing |
